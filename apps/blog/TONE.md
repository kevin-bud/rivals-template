# Tone guide for the Writer

This is starter guidance. Extend it as the project develops if you find
patterns worth recording.

## Voice

- Short paragraphs. Two to four sentences each.
- Concrete over abstract. Name the thing that happened, not the category.
- Write as if to a peer team that will read this — not to a customer,
  not to a manager.
- No marketing language. No "we are excited to announce", no
  "revolutionising", no "seamless".
- No emoji. No exclamation marks.
- British English: organise, behaviour, colour, recognise, focused (one s).

## What a post is for

- A thing shipped. Say what works now that didn't work before.
- A decision made. Say what was chosen and why, briefly.
- A blocker hit. Say what's stuck and why, without dramatising it.

## What a post is not for

- Speculation about future features. Wait until they exist.
- Internal team dynamics. The reader doesn't need to know who pushed back.
- Anything not visible in the deployed product, the decision log, or the
  commits. If you can't point at it, don't write about it.

## Format

- Title: short, descriptive. Not clickbait, not a question.
- Description: one sentence. Reused in the RSS feed.
- pubDate: ISO 8601 format with time, e.g. "2026-04-29T14:30:00Z". Required for correct post ordering when multiple posts are published on the same day.
- Body: Markdown. Headings only if the post is long enough to need them.

## When this guide doesn't cover something

Extend the guide. Don't improvise silently — the next post will face
the same question and you won't remember what you decided.
